What is AI?

You've heard the term everywhere. But what does AI actually do — and what does it mean for your business? No jargon. No hype. Just a clear, honest explanation in plain English.

The Simple Truth

AI in 3 Steps

Forget everything you've seen in movies. Here's what AI actually does — and why it's genuinely useful for your business.

01

You Give It Examples

You feed AI your historical data — past invoices, emails, customer records, sales history, support tickets. This is the raw material it learns from. The more relevant examples you provide, the smarter it becomes at your specific problem.

Example: 5 years of invoice data, flagged by your finance team as normal or suspicious.
02

It Finds the Patterns

AI is extraordinarily good at finding patterns in large datasets that humans would never notice. It's like hiring a new employee — but one who's read every invoice you've ever received and remembers every detail perfectly, without fatigue or bias.

It learns: Suspicious invoices often have round numbers, new vendors, and arrive on Fridays.
03

It Applies What It Learned

Now, every time a new invoice arrives, the AI checks it against what it learned and flags anything suspicious — automatically, in real time, without a human having to review it first. This is how AI delivers value: automating judgment at scale.

Result: Finance team only reviews the 3 flagged invoices — not all 10,000.
Real-World Example

You Already Use AI Every Day

The best example of AI in action is the one you use without thinking about it: Netflix's recommendation engine. It doesn't have a person reviewing your watch history. It found the patterns in what millions of people watch — and applies those patterns to suggest what you'll enjoy next.

Your business problems are exactly the same kind of problem. You have historical data. There are patterns in it. AI can find those patterns and apply them — faster, more accurately, and at a scale no human team can match.

Netflix Case Study

How Netflix's AI Works

Netflix doesn't guess what you'll like. Its AI learned from the viewing behaviour of hundreds of millions of subscribers — finding patterns like "people who watched X and Y usually love Z." Then it applies that learning to everyone's home screen, in real time.

STEP 1 Examples: 200M+ users' watch history, ratings, and behaviour
STEP 2 Patterns: Viewers who liked Stranger Things + Ozark tend to like…
STEP 3 Applies: Your home screen shows exactly what you're likely to watch next
AI in Your Business

What AI Can Do For You

The 3-step process applies to any business problem where you have historical data and a decision you need to make faster, more accurately, or at greater scale.

📧

Email & Document Processing

Feed AI thousands of emails and documents. It learns to route, categorise, summarise, and extract information automatically — saving your team from manual processing at scale.

💰

Invoice & Fraud Detection

AI learns the patterns of legitimate transactions vs suspicious ones. It flags anomalies automatically — protecting your business from fraud without slowing down payments.

📦

Demand Forecasting

Feed AI your sales history, seasonality data, and external signals. It learns the patterns and predicts what you'll need to stock, hire, or produce — weeks or months in advance.

🎯

Customer Churn Prediction

AI learns the behaviour patterns of customers who eventually leave — and flags at-risk accounts early, so your team can intervene before the cancellation happens.

💬

Customer Support Automation

Feed AI your previous support conversations and resolutions. It learns to answer common questions instantly and route complex ones — reducing your support volume dramatically.

📊

Business Intelligence

AI can analyse your operational data and surface non-obvious insights — the patterns in your sales data that explain why certain products outperform, or which customer segments are most valuable.
