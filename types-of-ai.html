Three Types
of AI

People talk about "AI" as if it's one thing. It isn't. There are three fundamentally different types — and they solve completely different business problems. Understanding the difference is the first step to knowing which one you actually need.

Next: AI-Led Automation → ← What is AI?
01
THE ANALYST

Traditional AI

Traditional AI finds patterns in structured data and makes predictions. It's the workhorse of the AI world — less glamorous than ChatGPT, but responsible for billions of dollars in business value through fraud detection, demand forecasting, credit scoring, and risk assessment.

It works on structured data — spreadsheets, databases, transaction logs. Feed it historical data, and it learns to predict outcomes: will this customer churn? Will this invoice be fraudulent? How much stock do I need next month?

Fraud detection — learns patterns of fraudulent transactions and flags new ones in milliseconds
Demand forecasting — predicts what you'll need to stock based on historical sales patterns
Credit scoring — assesses loan default risk based on thousands of financial variables
02
THE CREATOR

Generative AI

Generative AI doesn't just analyse — it creates. It can write text, answer questions, summarise documents, generate images, and produce reports. ChatGPT and Claude are the most famous examples. Unlike Traditional AI, it works on unstructured data — text, images, audio, video.

It's extraordinarily useful for knowledge work: summarising lengthy documents, drafting communications, answering questions from a knowledge base, creating content, and assisting with research.

Document Q&A — ask questions about thousands of contracts or reports in plain English
Report generation — automatically write summaries, analyses, and briefings from data
Email drafting — generate personalised customer communications at scale, maintaining brand voice
03
THE DOER — MOST POWERFUL

Agentic AI

Agentic AI doesn't just analyse or create — it takes action. It can plan multi-step tasks, use software tools, connect to your systems, and execute complex business processes end-to-end with minimal human intervention. This is the frontier of AI capability.

An AI agent can receive an instruction like "process all incoming invoices, match them to purchase orders, flag anomalies, and update the ERP" — and do it autonomously, across your entire technology stack, continuously.

Invoice processing — reads invoice, matches to PO, flags discrepancies, updates ERP, notifies finance
Monitoring & escalation — watches systems 24/7, detects anomalies, takes predefined action, escalates when needed
Improves with every run — learns from outcomes to handle more edge cases over time without retraining
